Reading Tarot With the Lunar Cycle

Reading tarot with the lunar cycle means allowing each phase of the moon to shape the intention you bring to the cards. You can still draw every day, once a month, or whenever you remember. The moon doesn't decide when you're allowed to read. She influences the energy of your questions and your intentions.

I've always loved the moon.

I say hello to her whenever I see her. Sometimes in the morning, pale and still hanging in the sky. Sometimes when she rises so large over the horizon that I stop whatever I'm doing to watch her.

I feel different when I can see her.

I know there are scientific explanations for some of this, and spiritual explanations for the rest. I'm not sure where one ends and the other begins. Maybe, for me, it was because I was born on a full moon, or because I used to send messages to people I couldn't talk to via her. All I know is that I've always felt connected to her.

She rules the tides. In astrology, she is connected to emotion, intuition and the body. Her cycle has also felt entwined with my own, in ways I noticed long before I had language for them.

So when I began building Enchantra, it made sense that she would be ruled by the moon too.

How I Read Tarot With the Lunar Cycle

I have never been habitual with tarot. I was always more intuitive. I should do a reading now, tonight, on the full moon.

If it was a new moon, however, I would set an intention for the cycle ahead. At the full moon, I would reflect on what had shifted.

I loved the ritual of it. The problem was that by the time the full moon arrived, I never remembered what I had asked at the new moon, or what any of my other readings were.

Sometimes I'd written it in a notebook I couldn't find. Sometimes it was in the notes app on an old phone. More often, I'd told myself it was important enough that I would remember it.

I did not.

So the full moon reading became a reflection on what I thought I had meant two weeks earlier. Which isn't quite the same thing.

That's one of the reasons I built Enchantra. She holds the intention, the cards and the reflections together, because I had years of wonderful intentions and almost no reliable record of any of them.

Since building her, my practice has changed.

I still set an intention at the new moon. I still return to it at the full moon. But now I also draw cards through the days between them, and I can see the patterns forming in between the cards.

Some mornings I draw a card to help focus my attention. What am I carrying today? What am I avoiding? What might I need to remember before I begin? What should I focus on?

Some days I forget. And this happens more often than not.

But when I do draw, the meaning now sits inside the larger intention of the lunar cycle. The daily reading isn't isolated anymore. It becomes part of a longer, deeper, more meaningful conversation.

Sometimes a pattern is obvious. Sometimes the cards seem completely unrelated until one word, one image or one repeated suit makes it all feel a bit too close to home.

And sometimes I still don't understand it. And that counts too.

Why the Moon Changes the Question

It is said the phases of the moon carry different energies. You don't have to believe they control your life to work with them. You can treat the lunar cycle as a rhythm.

The new moon begins in darkness. The light grows. The moon becomes full. Then the light recedes and the cycle begins again.

The moon creates, then witnesses, then transforms. Then it begins again.

Those movements can help shape the kind of intention you bring to tarot.

At a new moon, I might ask: "What am I ready to begin?"

During the waxing moon: "What am I building?"

At the full moon: "What is being illuminated?"

During the waning moon: "What am I ready to release?"

The cards don't have to predict what each phase will bring. They help you notice how you're moving through it.

What This Isn't

The moon doesn't make your tarot reading more accurate.

It doesn't guarantee that your intention will unfold in the way you want.

A difficult card isn't a warning that something terrible will happen during that phase. The Tower at a new moon doesn't mean the month is cursed.

Tarot doesn't replace real help. If you're depressed, see a therapist. If you're in financial trouble, speak to a financial counsellor. If you're unsafe, contact someone who can help you become safe.

The lunar cycle can hold your attention. It can't hold your life together for you.

The Moon Holds the Thread

Every time you return to an intention, you're choosing to stay with it a little longer.

You're choosing to notice what happened between the question and the answer.

You're choosing to let the daily cards become part of something larger.

You're choosing to remember what you asked before your mind edits it into something easier.

The moon keeps changing. So do you.

If you take one thing today: let the moon shape the question, then notice what changes before she becomes full again.
